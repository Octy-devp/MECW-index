## 2. CORE THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK

Marx and Engels developed an integrated body of thought. Key concepts:

### Historical Materialism
- The mode of production conditions social, political, and intellectual life
- History as the history of class struggles
- Base (economic structure) and superstructure (state, ideology, culture)

### Critique of Political Economy
- Commodity fetishism: social relations appear as relations between things
- Surplus value: the source of capitalist profit extracted from labor
- Law of the tendency of the rate of profit to fall
- Primitive accumulation and the genesis of capital

### Class Struggle and Revolution
- The proletariat as the revolutionary class
- Dictatorship of the proletariat as transition to classless society
- The state as instrument of class rule, to wither away under communism

### Dialectics
- Unity and struggle of opposites
- Quantitative change → qualitative transformation
- Negation of the negation

## 3. HISTORICAL PERIODS

1830s: Youth and education. Marx at university, Engels in Bremen.
1840s: Revolutionary journalism. Rheinische Zeitung, German Ideology,
       Communist Manifesto (1848). Year of Revolutions.
1850s: London exile. New York Tribune correspondence. 1857 crisis analysis.
1860s: Capital Vol.1 (1867). First International (1864-1872).
1870s: Paris Commune (1871). Critique of the Gotha Programme (1875).
       Anti-Dühring (1877-78).
1880s: Marx's death (1883). Engels edits Capital Vol.2-3.
       Origin of the Family (1884). Second International foundation.
1890s: Engels' late letters on historical materialism. His death (1895).

## 7. DCA ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

For each document, apply the DCA (Crisis-Delay-Alternative-Direction) method:

**Crisis** — What specific historical crisis does this text respond to?
  (e.g., 1848 revolution defeat, 1857 economic crisis, 1871 Commune suppression)

**Delay (Lag)** — What structural lag does the text diagnose?
  (e.g., proletariat not yet organized, theory lagging behind practice,
   bourgeois ideology blocking class consciousness)

**Alternative** — What alternative path or solution does the text propose?
  (e.g., workers' party, dictatorship of proletariat, cooperative production)

**Direction** — What material constraints shape the direction proposed?
  (e.g., level of productive forces, existing class composition, geopolitical situation)
