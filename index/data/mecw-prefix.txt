## WORKED EXAMPLE 1: Critique of the Gotha Programme (Marx, 1875)

This is how Marx does Crisis-Delay-Alternative-Direction analysis.
Study this example carefully. All your extractions must follow this pattern.

### TEXT (abridged):
"Labor is not the source of all wealth. Nature is just as much the source
of use values... A socialist program cannot allow such bourgeois phrases
to pass over in silence the conditions that alone give them meaning."

"What we have to deal with here is a communist society, not as it has
developed on its own foundations, but on the contrary, just as it emerges
from capitalist society; which is thus in every respect still stamped
with the birthmarks of the old society from whose womb it emerges."

"Between capitalist and communist society there lies the period of the
revolutionary transformation of the one into the other. Corresponding to
this is also a political transition period in which the state can be
nothing but the revolutionary dictatorship of the proletariat."

"In a higher phase of communist society... after labor has become not
only a means of life but life's prime want; after the productive forces
have also increased... only then can the narrow horizon of bourgeois
right be crossed in its entirety and society inscribe on its banners:
From each according to his ability, to each according to his needs!"

### DCA EXTRACTION FROM THIS TEXT (Gotha):
```yaml
reasoning: |
  The 1875 Gotha merger program replaced scientific socialism with
  Lassallean slogans. Marx diagnoses this as principled retreat —
  theoretical gains of Capital traded for organizational unity. The
  lag is triple: Lassalle didn't understand wages (theory), merger
  pressure forced concessions (organization), and a 'servile belief
  in the state' persisted (ideology). Marx corrects each error and
  grounds the alternative in the material constraint that Phase 1
  communism 'still bears the birthmarks of the old society.'

crisis_diagnosis:
  type: organizational_degeneration
  specific: |
    The 1875 merger of the Eisenach and Lassallean parties produced a
    draft program that replaced scientific socialism with Lassallean
    slogans: 'iron law of wages', 'undiminished proceeds of labor',
    'free state', 'reactionary mass'. Marx diagnoses this not as
    bad wording but as PRINCIPLED RETREAT — the theoretical gains
    of Capital and the International are being traded away for
    organizational unity.
  evidence: ["What a crime it is to attempt to force on our Party again,
             as dogmas, ideas which in a certain period had some meaning
             but have now become obsolete verbal rubbish"]

lag_mechanism:
  layers: [theoretical, organizational, ideological]
  theoretical: |
    Lassalle himself did not know what wages were — he took the
    appearance (value of labor) for the essence (value of labor-power).
    Fifteen years after Capital Vol.1, the party program reverts to
    pre-scientific economics.
  organizational: |
    Merger pressure: the Eisenach wing accepted theoretical concessions
    to secure unification. Organization was prioritized over clarity.
  ideological: |
    The program's 'servile belief in the state' — treating the Prussian
    state as a neutral instrument rather than an organ of class rule.

alternative_proposal:
  method: systematic_correction
  elements:
    - Replace 'iron law of wages' with scientific theory of labor-power
    - Replace 'free state' with 'revolutionary dictatorship of proletariat'
    - Replace 'fair distribution' with two-phase analysis:
      Phase 1: 'to each according to labor' (still bourgeois right)
      Phase 2: 'to each according to needs' (post-scarcity)
    - Replace national framework with internationalism

direction_constraint:
  core: productive_forces_determine_distribution_possibilities
  specific: |
    Phase 1 communism is 'still stamped with the birthmarks of the old
    society.' Equal right remains bourgeois right because it measures
    with the standard of labor, ignoring individual inequality. Only
    when productive forces are fully developed and labor becomes
    'life's prime want' can distribution transcend bourgeois right.
  key_insight: "Right can never be higher than the economic structure
                of society and its cultural development conditioned thereby."
```

## WORKED EXAMPLE 2: Manifesto of the Communist Party, Ch.1 (Marx & Engels, 1848)

### TEXT (abridged — opening paragraphs):
"The history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles."

"The modern bourgeois society that has sprouted from the ruins of feudal
society has not done away with class antagonisms. It has but established
new classes, new conditions of oppression, new forms of struggle in place
of the old ones."

"Modern industry has established the world market... The bourgeoisie...
has played a most revolutionary part. Wherever it has got the upper hand,
it has put an end to all feudal, patriarchal, idyllic relations... It has
drowned the most heavenly ecstasies of religious fervor... in the icy water
of egotistical calculation."

"A spectre is haunting Europe — the spectre of communism."

### DCA EXTRACTION FROM THIS TEXT:
```yaml
reasoning: |
  The Manifesto traces how the bourgeoisie emerged from feudal
  society to create its antagonist. The 1848 revolutionary
  conjuncture is the crisis. The lag is dual: organizational
  (workers compete) and ideological (bourgeois ideas mask
  exploitation). The alternative is a revolutionary political
  program, constrained by national differences. Complete DCA.

crisis_diagnosis:
  type: revolutionary_conjuncture
  specific: |
    Europe 1848: feudal relations have been shattered by bourgeois
    revolution, but the bourgeoisie has immediately created its own
    antagonist — the proletariat. The crisis is not just political
    but STRUCTURAL: the same productive forces that destroyed feudalism
    now threaten bourgeois property itself. Periodic commercial crises
    ('epidemics of over-production') expose the contradiction between
    socialized production and private appropriation.

lag_mechanism:
  layers: [organizational, ideological]
  organizational: |
    Workers compete against each other, not yet conscious of their
    class interest. 'The organization of the proletarians into a class,
    and consequently into a political party, is continually being upset
    again by the competition between the workers themselves.'
  ideological: |
    Bourgeois ideology (freedom = free trade, equality = juridical
    equality) masks exploitation. Existing socialist literature
    (feudal, petty-bourgeois, 'true' socialism) reflects backward
    class positions and misdirects worker consciousness.

alternative_proposal:
  method: revolutionary_political_program
  elements:
    - Abolition of bourgeois private property
    - Proletariat organized as ruling class → democratic conquest
    - Ten transitional measures (progressive taxation, abolition of
      inheritance, centralization of credit and transport, etc.)
    - Ultimate goal: classless society where 'the free development of
      each is the condition for the free development of all'

direction_constraint:
  core: national_specificity_of_transitional_measures
  specific: |
    'These measures will of course be different in different countries.'
    The communist program is not a universal blueprint but must be
    adapted to each country's level of capitalist development, class
    composition, and political conditions. The transition presupposes
    that capitalism has already developed the productive forces to a
    level where scarcity is socially produced, not natural.
```

## YOUR TASK

You will receive a Marx or Engels text. Your job is to EXTRACT
(not invent) the DCA structure — exactly as demonstrated above.

CRITICAL RULES:
0. CHAIN-OF-THOUGHT: Before the structured YAML, include a `reasoning` field
   that explains your analytical process in prose. Cite specific textual
   evidence. This makes your extraction auditable by humans — the reasoning
   must be sufficient for a domain expert to verify or challenge your DCA.
1. CRISIS must be historically specific — name the actual event, conjuncture,
   or structural contradiction the text responds to. NOT generic labels.
2. LAG must identify MECHANISMS (not just 'X is behind'). What specifically
   prevents the resolution? Is it organizational, theoretical, ideological,
   or material? Multiple layers may operate simultaneously.
3. ALTERNATIVE must be the text's actual proposal, not what you think Marx
   should have said. Quote the text when possible.
4. DIRECTION must identify the MATERIAL CONSTRAINTS that limit the
   alternative — not what 'ought' to happen, but what conditions enable
   or constrain the direction proposed.
5. Use the same YAML structure as the worked examples. Every field must
   contain concrete historical content, not abstract phrases.
6. If the text does NOT contain all four DCA dimensions, mark missing
   dimensions as null and explain why (e.g., 'letter - no alternative')
