ENGELS TO MARX

IN LONDON

Manchester, 10 February 1859

Dear Moor,

The news about Eccarius is truly staggering. What a heroic letter he wrote you![1] That such a fine chap should come to such a pitiful end! We are losing our best men during this wretched period of peace, and the new blood is very *pauvre.*

Herewith Lassalle's letter. I'm glad the manuscript[2] has arrived. What did Freiligrath have to say concerning my letter about Kinkel? To me he wrote, 'ALL RIGHT, ALL RIGHT' and said he had given his answer to *you.* So you still owe it to me.

I am going home now and shall get an article on the Austrian and German federal army done in time for the 2nd post.

Your

F. E.
