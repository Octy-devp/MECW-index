ENGELS TO HERMANN ENGELS

IN BARMEN

[London,] 17 February 1892

Dear Hermann,

It will be perfectly all right if you send me the statement *before* 30 April, in which case perhaps you would be good enough to notify me *by postcard* when the relevant dividends have been paid in and how much they amount to. That is the best way of dealing with it.

Since yesterday we have been having glorious German winter weather, cold and snowy. I was in Richmond today, visiting an old friend[1] who is ill, and then in town. The weather has really toned up my nerves and the Pschorrbräu I drank afterwards tasted marvellous. Love to Emma[2] and all the others.

Your

Friedrich
