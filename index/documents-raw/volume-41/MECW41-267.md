ENGELS TO MARX

IN LONDON

Manchester, 7 January 1863[1]

Dear Moor,

Mary[2] is dead. Last night she went to bed early and, when Lizzy[3] wanted to go to bed shortly before midnight, she found she had already died. Quite suddenly. Heart failure or an apoplectic stroke. I wasn't told till this morning; on Monday evening^K she was still quite well. I simply can't convey what I feel. The poor girl loved me with all her heart.

Your

F. E.
