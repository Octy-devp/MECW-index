MARX TO JUSTE VERNOUILLET[1]

IN PARIS

[London,] 12 July 1875

Dear Citizen,

You have done me a great service in telling me all about the affair. I shall at once write to M. Lahure, asking him to let me have an unequivocal reply.[2]

In the meantime, perhaps you would be so kind as to send me the name and address of that *managerial* Cerberus,[3] as also the address of a *lawyer* to whom I might have recourse should need arise.

If the French government wants a rumpus, I have sufficient contacts with the European press to do it signal service in that respect.

Yours very sincerely,

K. Marx[4]
