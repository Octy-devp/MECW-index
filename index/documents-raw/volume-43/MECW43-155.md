ENGELS TO MARX

IN LONDON

[Manchester,] 17 February 1869

Dear Moor,

I shall have the report[1] READY for you by next Tuesday,[2] though I don't know what interests you mainly. Enclosed is a letter from Lugau.[3]

Best thanks for the Westbury story.[4] I return, enclosed, the letter to Borkheim.[5] I shall ask my SHAREBROKER about it.

Enclosed is the extract made by the illustrious Gaudissart^8 about Bakunin,[6] together with annotations. You see that the main strong passages, which he quoted to us in Russian, only exist in his imagination. Still, the Pan-Slavism is really spread rather too thickly, in particular the threats against the Poles are significant. And the dissolution of the Russian Empire is qualified by the fact that Great Russia should still become the centre of the Slav confederation.

Your

F. E.
