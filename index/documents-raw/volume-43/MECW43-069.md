MARX TO HERMANN JUNG

IN LONDON

London, 14 September 1868

Dear Jung,

*The Times* today carries Eccarius' reports of the 9th and 10th.[1]

In the debate on machinery, he makes Lessner's SPEECH *worse;* it was much better in *The Daily News.* Lessner quoted my book,[2] as you have seen in *The Daily News.*[3] Eccarius *suppresses* the quotation.

Yet even better. In *The Daily News* the resolution about machines, etc., was noted as a *proposal from the GENERAL COUNCIL.* In *The Times,* Mr Eccarius converts this into his *personal proposal.*

This is a point on which you must collar him. His egotism needs a Cuff TO SET HIM RIGHT AGAIN.

Yours

K. M.
