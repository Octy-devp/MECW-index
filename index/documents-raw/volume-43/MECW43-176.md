ENGELS TO MARX

IN LONDON

Manchester, 2 April 1869

Dear Moor,

I cannot write much to you today, since for some time I have had a chronically inflamed eye (mild), and can only do the most essential work.

*L'homme propose et la femme dispose.*[1][2] Yesterday evening I laid out for you a pile of copies of *Zukunft,* and also B. Becker on Lassalle,[3] but this morning the room was being swept out, so I forgot them. *Die Zukunft,* incidentally, has carried the Lugau story as a feature article.[4]

Wilhelm's stupidities exceed everything. Bebel's cry of alarm was really touching.[5] As you will see from *Zukunft,*[6] his friends in Hanover, the particularists, voted in the Lüneburg election *against* Yorck (Lassallean) and *for* the National-Liberal.[7] The rabble are all united against the workers but this does not disturb Liebknecht. I look forward to the dread debate with great eagerness.

About *Vogt*:[8] if I were you I would write directly to the Berlin Hirsch. Then you will finally reach the proper quarter.

Lizzie is better, but does not go out yet and is on a strengthening diet. How is MRS Lafargue?

Best wishes.

Your

F. E.
