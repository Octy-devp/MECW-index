ENGELS TO MARX

IN LONDON

Manchester, 19 May 1869

Dear Moor,

I must congratulate you on not having come here last week in this lousy weather. But this time you should really come.

About the *Peasant War* when we meet. The address about Belgium is very good, and that for the Americans even better.[1] I saw them in the *Bee-Hive*.[2]

Please bring with you the two numbers of the Prussian General Staff Report of 1866;[3] I must compare them with the Austrian ones, which show up some pretty Prussian exaggerations.

Let me know as soon as possible when you will be coming. Your liver must have got somewhat better in the meantime. Close of post.

Your

F. E.
